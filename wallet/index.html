Concept: wallet app

This mobile app makes paying, lending and borrowing money more convenient. I designed it during my Master in Interaction Design and it was awarded by Umeå University as a good idea for entrepeneurship.

Concept · UX design

The idea behind this app came from talking to people in the Payment Cards industry and other friends about how they use their money, what differences they experience between using cards and cash, and what they would love to be able to do.

In Sweden, most people use credit or debit cards to make their payments. In terms of usage, there is a great difference between Sweden and countries like Spain or England, in which cash is still very popular. The insights from talking with people in Sweden is that, although using only cards is more convenient than carrying cash everywhere, they miss some of the control that they had over cash: they cannot lend or borrow the money quickly to/from friends and family - even when their friend is next to them -, for example, being in a coffee shop or buying clothes. Many people also replied that they feel closer to their money if it is cash, as they can touch it.

I wanted to create an app that lets you have the convenience of using payments cards - like being weightless, being able to carry more money, being more secure - with these actions that only cash is able to provide, so you can feel closer to it.

The app is very simple. It looks like a phone or messaging app but, instead of calling or sending messages, you can send and receive money that is stored in your bank account.

You can do all of the actions described above. In addition, anybody is able to set their profile as public so it is shown in other apps nearby. This is very useful for stores to be able to receive payments without the need of adding the contact previously. For customers, buying this way is quicker and more convenient than even paying with card. It allows them to avoid queues.
